21/*
22 * Security protocols certificate interface. This is used by security protocols to
23 * access certificate information.
24 *
25 * Own certificate chain contains the certificate chain that is sent on TLS handshake
26 * to remote end. Typically this is one certificate long, and the certificate chains
27 * to root CA certificate or to intermediate certificate known to other end. It is
28 * also possible to send chain longer than one certificate.
29 *
30 * Key on own certificate chain must be the private key of the certificate used on
31 * TLS handshake.
32 *
33 * Trusted certificate chains contains the root CA certificates and intermediate
34 * certificates chains that are used to validate remote certificates.
35 *
36 */
37
